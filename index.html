The trap we're breaking

A poverty, health, and deforestation trap

Most Haitian families cook over charcoal and wood. That single dependence drives three crises at once.

  • Health. Daily smoke causes respiratory disease, hitting women and children hardest.
  • Budgets. Buying fuel strains already-stretched family incomes.
  • Forests. Cutting wood for fuel accelerates deforestation across the country.
The sun-powered answer

Cooking with light, not smoke

Solar cookers turn free sunlight into safe, smokeless heat. We don't just hand them out — we teach, demonstrate, and build a local market so the change lasts.

  • No smoke, no fuel cost — sunlight is free and clean.
  • Built and sold locally to create jobs and keep money in Haiti.
  • Backed by data from cellphone surveys with real families.

How it works

From a single demonstration to a lasting habit

Adoption spreads when families see results with their own eyes. Here's the path from first spark to everyday use.

Educate & train

Through Haiti's first university course on solar cooking and hands-on sessions, we equip local teachers, students, and entrepreneurs.

Demonstrate in community

Our Haitian team cooks live at schools, churches, and gatherings — prioritizing institutions serving children and adolescent girls.

Make it affordable

Cookers are sold far below cost, encouraging entrepreneurship while reaching the lowest-income households.

Measure & improve

Cellphone surveys capture each family's experience, feeding innovation and local production.
