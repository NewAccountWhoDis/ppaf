Who We Are

People who innovate and collaborate

The Public-Private Alliance Foundation brings together leaders in development, finance, public health, and diplomacy — united by a belief that the sun can change daily life in Haiti.

Our mission

Innovate, collaborate, and advance solar cooking

PPAF advances solar cooking technology to break Haiti's poverty–respiratory disease–deforestation trap. We introduce and monitor sun-powered cookstoves through partnerships with Haitian and US-based colleagues.

We work as a bridge — connecting public institutions, private enterprise, and local communities so that a clean way to cook becomes a normal way to cook.

Board & Management

Leadership

A volunteer board with deep roots in international development, finance, public health, and diplomacy.

David Stillman

Executive Director & Board Member (Ex Officio)

Founder of PPAF who has led project activities in Madagascar, the Dominican Republic, and Haiti. A former UN career spanned rural development and intergovernmental cooperation. BA, American University; MA & PhD in political science, Duke University.

Donna Rosa

Board Chair

Founder & Chief Entrepreneurship Officer at EFour Enterprises LLC. A nutrition and food-science background plus an MBA, applied to micro-enterprise across Africa, the Caribbean, and the Middle East. Received IFT's Humanitarian Award in 2023.

Eustache Clerveaux

Treasurer

Senior Financial Planner at Hudson Financial Group and adjunct finance professor at NYU; previously Morgan Stanley and JPMorgan Chase. CFP® and CPWA®. A native of Haiti committed to community service and financial literacy.

Carol J. Dabbs

Board Member

Former Senior Coordinator for U.S. Foreign Assistance at the State Department and a USAID veteran specializing in public-health strategy across 25+ countries. BA, Duke University; MPH, UNC Chapel Hill.

Amb. Zina Andrianarivelo-Razafy

Board Member

Madagascar's Permanent Representative to the UN (2002–2018) and Ambassador to the US, World Bank, and IMF. MBA, Ball State University. Fluent in Malagasy, French, English, and German.

Jenny Lee Platek

Accountant

A volunteer since 2019 with 30+ years in financial services. BS, Cornell University; MBA, NYU Stern.

In memoriam

Jeanne Betsock "Ginger" Stillman

Founder & Secretary · 1939–2024

Ginger designed health and project-management training programs across 20 countries and edited an 11-volume training series. MSPH, UNC Chapel Hill; BA, American University. She was included in multiple Who's Who editions, and her vision remains at the heart of PPAF's work. She passed in August 2024.

Our colleagues

The people who carry the work forward

PPAF is a network, not just a board. Colleagues in Haiti lead demonstrations, training, travel, outreach, and local problem-solving, supported by volunteers in the United States.

Hinche, Haiti

Building and teaching

The Hinche team supports locally made cookers, demonstrations, training, and the JBS Workshop named in Ginger Stillman's memory.

Jacmel, Haiti

Demonstrating and organizing

Colleagues in Jacmel introduce families and young people to solar cooking and build a community of future cooker owners.

United States

Connecting skills and support

US-based colleagues contribute fundraising, communications, technical knowledge, partnerships, and links to international institutions.

Our history

From global development to Haiti's kitchens

PPAF grew out of decades of international development experience, refined into one focused mission.

Founded on partnership

Established to bridge public institutions and private enterprise for development that lasts.

Across three countries

Project activities in Madagascar, the Dominican Republic, and Haiti built the experience behind today's work.

Focused on solar cooking

PPAF concentrated its mission on sun-powered cooking in Haiti — including the country's first university course on the subject.

Vetted & growing

A UN Global Compact participant and GlobalGiving vetted organization, working with partners worldwide.
